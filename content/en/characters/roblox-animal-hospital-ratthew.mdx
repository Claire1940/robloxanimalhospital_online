<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Anomaly Detection Guide">

Spotting anomalies is your shield against the terrors of the night shift. Use this structured approach to screen every visitor who approaches the reception counter.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Perform a Visual Check">
    Look directly at the patient at the desk. Inspect their facial structure, eyes, mouth, and posture. Watch for twitching animations, hollow eyes, a wide grin, or realistic human-like teeth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Verify via CCTV Cameras">
    Switch to the lobby and check-in cameras. Some anomalies look perfectly normal in person but display distorted limbs, pitch-black bodies, or black boxes over their eyes when viewed through the monitor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Compare the Patient Photo">
    Check the file photo on your desk. Reject the patient immediately if the photo does not match their current appearance, if the photo has a static grainy effect, or if the photo displays bloodshot eyes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Activate the Shutters">
    If any of the checks reveal an anomaly, press the shutter button at your desk to send them away. If the patient passes all three checks, safely admit them to the treatment rooms.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Threat Warning">
If an anomaly is accidentally admitted, it can morph into a Skinwalker. When this happens, coordinate with your team to locate the threat, use a Taser or Gun to neutralize it, and consume Coffee to restore lost sanity.
</Callout>

</Section>
