<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Survival Guide for Your First Shift">

Succeeding in your first few shifts requires a methodical approach. Follow these precise steps to secure your first clean run and earn your initial batch of Animal Coins.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Report to the Reception Desk">
    As soon as the shift begins, head straight to the front desk. This is where patients arrive. Your primary task here is to decide whether the visitor is a normal animal or a dangerous anomaly trying to slip inside.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Perform the Triple-Check Inspection">
    When a patient approaches, do not accept them immediately. First, look at them directly for physical deformities. Second, check their photo ID on the desk. Third, look at the CCTV monitor to spot camera-only anomalies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reject Anomalies and Accept Patients">
    If the animal passes all checks, let them in. If you spot even one anomaly clue, press the shutter button immediately to reject them. Correctly rejecting anomalies prevents internal outbreaks and protects your team.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Accurate Medical Treatments">
    Accepted patients go to rooms 1 through 8. Read the diagnostic screen in their room, complete the mini-game (like the X-Ray sequence or Heart Monitor clicks), and apply the exact medicine required.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Manage Sanity and Complete the Shift">
    Keep an eye on your Sanity meter. If it drops, drink Coffee or eat shop food. Once all patients are treated or safely rejected and the timer runs out, you will successfully complete the shift.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Giving the wrong treatment item to a normal patient will kill them instantly. This severely lowers your final shift score and wastes valuable hospital resources.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="How to Spot and Reject Anomalies">

Anomaly detection is the most important skill in the game. Anomalies present themselves in various subtle and obvious ways. You must check the patient in person, on their photo, and through the CCTV system to catch every threat.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="In-Person Signs">
    - **Physical Deformities**: Extra eyes, hollow sockets, or human-like teeth.
    - **Unnatural Movements**: Severe body twitching or distorted grins.
    - **Action**: Reject immediately using the front desk shutters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="CCTV Monitor Signs">
    - **Visual Glitches**: Black boxes over eyes or completely void-black bodies.
    - **Distorted Limbs**: Elongated legs or arms visible only on camera.
    - **Action**: Trust the camera feed over your physical eyes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Photo ID Signs">
    - **Mismatched Photos**: The species or features do not match the live patient.
    - **Cursed Images**: Static grain, bloodshot eyes, or terrifying expressions.
    - **Action**: Reject the patient if the photo feels corrupted.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

| Anomaly Category | Common Visual Clues | Immediate Counteraction | Severity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Physical** | Sharp teeth, wide grins, three eyes, twitching | Close reception shutters | High |
| **Camera-Only** | Stretched limbs, staring at camera, black void body | Trust CCTV, reject patient | High |
| **Photo-Based** | Grainy static, bloodshot eyes, mismatching species | Compare photo with live animal | High |
| **Environmental** | Green floor slime, Bed Monsters, Wall Bangers | Use Fire Extinguisher, avoid looking | Medium |

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
If an anomaly slips past the desk, it can transform into a hostile Skinwalker. If this happens, you must use self-defense weapons like Tasers or Guns to neutralize the threat before it kills your team.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Sanity Management & Treatment Items">

Sanity is your true health bar. Staring at cursed photos, letting anomalies inside, or facing environmental monsters will rapidly drain your Sanity. If it reaches zero, you die. Use the table below to learn which items protect your mind and cure your patients.

| Item Name | Category | Primary Use Case | Common Mistake to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coffee** | Sanity Recovery | Restores Sanity during long, intense shifts | Drinking it too early before taking damage |
| **Taser** | Threat Control | Temporarily stops hostile Skinwalkers | Wasting charges on non-hostile entities |
| **Fire Extinguisher** | Emergency Tool | Puts out room fires and burning patients | Forgetting to apply Ointment after the fire |
