<Section icon={Search} title="Anomaly Detection: The Solo Triple-Check">

For any player following this **roblox animal hospital solo guide**, the most critical skill is identifying anomalies. Admitting an anomaly into the hospital creates a Skinwalker, which will actively hunt you and drain your Sanity. As a solo player, you cannot afford to have a Skinwalker roaming the halls while you are trying to perform surgery in Room 8.

You must implement a strict "Triple-Check" routine for every visitor that arrives at the front desk. Do not rush this process, even if the waiting line is growing. A single mistake is more time-consuming to fix than a 10-second delay in check-in.

**The Triple-Check Routine:**
1.  **Visual Inspection**: Look for obvious physical deformities like extra eyes, realistic human teeth, or a distorted grin.
2.  **CCTV Verification**: Check the monitor. Some anomalies only reveal their true form on camera, appearing as dark voids or having limbs that stretch unnaturally.
3.  **Photo Comparison**: Compare the patient's current appearance with their provided photo. If the ears, nose, or eye color differ even slightly, it is an anomaly.

| Anomaly Category | Common Visual Tell | Required Action |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Physical Distortion** | Three eyes / Sharp human teeth / Wide grins | Immediate Rejection |
| **Camera Anomalies** | Black box over eyes / Twitching on CCTV only | Immediate Rejection |
| **Photo Mismatch** | Photo shows different animal or features | Immediate Rejection |
| **Environmental** | Patient staring directly into the CCTV lens | Immediate Rejection |
| **Aggressive** | Patient growling or making human-like noises | Immediate Rejection |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
If a patient looks normal but the photo has bloodshot eyes or a "cursed" grain effect, do not admit them. Staring at cursed photos for too long will also drain your Sanity.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Solo Shift Walkthrough">

To maximize your efficiency, follow this structured workflow for every night shift. This sequence ensures you maintain control over the hospital environment while progressing through the patient queue.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Preparation">
    Before the first patient arrives, check the shop. If you have enough cash, buy a **Coffee** and a **Taser**. Position yourself at the front desk and ensure your CCTV monitor is functioning.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Batch Check-In">
    Check in 2-3 patients at once. Use the Triple-Check method to reject anomalies immediately. Admitting a small batch allows you to move to the treatment area and finish multiple tasks in one trip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Rapid Diagnosis & Treatment">
    Move to the treatment rooms. Interact with the computers to diagnose the animal. Collect the required items (Ointment, Eye Drops, etc.) and apply them quickly. For Room 8 (Surgery), focus entirely on the mini-game until finished.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Environmental Cleanup">
    Between patient batches, check for hazards. Look under beds for the Bed Monster, clear any green slime, and ensure no Wall Bangers are blocking your path. If your Sanity is below 40%, drink your Coffee.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Shift Completion">
    Once the required number of patients is treated or the timer expires, return to the front desk for the final tally. Use your earned Animal Coins to upgrade your class before starting the next night.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Efficiency Note">
Do not try to treat every patient one by one. Grouping your tasks saves time spent walking back and forth, which is the biggest time-sink in solo play.
</Callout>

In solo runs, your inventory space is limited. You must prioritize items that keep you alive over items that only help patients. A dead doctor cannot heal anyone.

**Priority Item List for Solo Players:**
- **Coffee**: Tier 1. Essential for Sanity recovery.
- **Taser**: Tier 1. Your only defense against missed anomalies.
- **Fire Extinguisher**: Tier 2. Necessary for random fire events.
- **Speed Cola**: Tier 3. Helpful for large hospitals but not mandatory.
- **Medical Supplies**: Tier 3. Only grab these when you are actively heading to a treatment room.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Mistakes">
