Identification is a multi-step process. You must analyze the patient at the desk, check the lobby cameras, and verify their identity against the provided photo. Any discrepancy is a signal that you are dealing with an enemy.

| Detection Method | What to Look For | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Physical Inspection** | Extra eyes, sharp teeth, realistic human-like features, or twitching limbs. | **High** |
| **CCTV Monitor** | Black boxes over eyes, distorted limbs, or the patient staring at the camera. | **Critical** |
| **Photo Comparison** | The photo shows a different animal or has been "cursed" with bloodshot eyes. | **Medium** |
| **Behavioral Cues** | Extreme aggression at the desk or ignoring verbal prompts from the staff. | **High** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Detection Warning">
Never rely on just one check. Some advanced anomalies look perfectly normal at the desk but reveal their true form only on the CCTV monitor. Always perform the "Triple Check" (Desk, Camera, Photo) before opening the internal doors.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Skinwalker Threat: Admitted Enemy Consequences">

The most feared **roblox animal hospital enemies** are the Skinwalkers. These entities do not spawn naturally; they are the direct result of a player admitting an anomaly into the hospital. Once an anomaly is placed in a treatment room, it has a high chance of transforming into a Skinwalker, which will then hunt players and attack legitimate patients.

To handle a Skinwalker, you must act quickly before the entire hospital's Sanity is depleted. Use the following steps to neutralize the threat:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Confirm the Breach">
    If you hear strange growling or see a patient has disappeared from their room, a Skinwalker is loose. Check the hallways immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip Defensive Tools">

  <Step num="3" title="Stun and Neutralize">

<Section icon={Target} title="Environmental Hazards and Room-Specific Enemies">

Not all **roblox animal hospital enemies** walk through the front door. Some are environmental hazards or specialized entities that haunt specific areas of the clinic. Managing these is just as important as catching anomalies at the reception desk.

| Enemy Name | Location | Behavior | Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bed Monster** | Under treatment beds | Grabs players who stand still near beds. | Stay moving / Use Taser. |
| **Wall Banger** | Near front desk | Makes loud banging noises to drain Sanity. | Ignore the noise / Use Coffee. |
| **Ceiling Stalker** | Hallways | Punishes players for looking up. | Keep your camera leveled. |
| **Green Slime** | Floors | Slows movement and causes minor damage. | Avoid the puddles. |
| **Wall Anomalies** | Corridors | Reach out from walls to steal items. | Walk in the center of the hall. |

These hazards increase in frequency as the shift progresses. By Shift 5 and beyond, you can expect multiple environmental threats to trigger simultaneously, requiring high coordination among the staff.
