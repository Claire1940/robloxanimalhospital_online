<Section icon={Shield} title="Roblox Animal Hospital Anomalies: Detection Masterlist">

Identifying **roblox animal hospital anomalies** is the most critical skill for any player. These entities mimic normal animal patients but possess subtle, disturbing differences. Detection requires a multi-step verification process: physical inspection, photo comparison, and CCTV monitoring. Some anomalies are obvious, such as those with three eyes, while others require you to spot a slight twitch or a "black box" covering their face on the camera feed.

| Anomaly Type | Physical Signs | CCTV/Photo Signs | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Visual Mimic** | Three eyes, sharp teeth, wide grin | Photo shows bloodshot eyes | **Reject Immediately** |
| **CCTV Phantom** | Looks normal in person | Limbs are stretched or distorted | **Reject Immediately** |
| **The Twitcher** | Constant unnatural head shaking | Static effect on camera feed | **Reject Immediately** |
| **Void Stalker** | Eyes are completely hollow | Patient stares directly at camera | **Reject Immediately** |
| **Skinwalker** | Appears after a missed check-in | Mimics a player or staff member | **Neutralize with Taser** |

When a patient approaches the desk, follow a strict routine. Do not admit anyone until you have checked the computer photo and cycled through the lobby cameras. If the photo looks "cursed" (distorted or bloody), it is a guaranteed anomaly. Be aware that staring at these cursed photos for too long will actively drain your Sanity.

<Callout type="tip" title="Detection Tip">
If you are unsure, wait a few seconds. Some anomalies only reveal their true nature through a sudden twitch or a change in facial expression that occurs after the initial interaction.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Sanity Management and Survival Tactics">

Sanity is your most precious resource. In the world of **roblox animal hospital anomalies**, your mental state determines whether you survive the night. Sanity drops when you encounter hostile events, look at cursed objects, or admit an anomaly by mistake. If your Sanity hits zero, your character dies, and the shift ends for you.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor the Meter">
    Keep a constant eye on your Sanity levels. Avoid staring at suspicious CCTV feeds or cursed photos for longer than necessary.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Use Recovery Items">
    Coffee is the primary tool for restoring Sanity. Use it sparingly during early hours and save it for high-stress events like rituals or Skinwalker attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Class Synergies">
    If playing in a team, ensure at least one person is playing a Sanity-restoring class like the Surgeon or Doctor to keep the group stable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Avoid Environmental Hazards">
    Interact with Wall Bangers and Bed Monsters only when necessary. These environmental threats are designed to chip away at your Sanity over time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Managing Sanity is not just about recovery; it's about prevention. Experienced players learn to "glance" at photos rather than study them. They also prioritize neutralizing Skinwalkers quickly, as their presence in the hospital causes a passive Sanity drain on everyone nearby.

| Risk Factor | Sanity Impact | Best Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cursed Photo** | Low/Continuous | Quick Glance |
| **Missed Anomaly** | High/Burst | Taser/Gun |
| **Wall Bangers** | Moderate | Keep Distance |
| **Death Ritual** | Critical | Clear Candles Fast |

<Callout type="info" title="Pro Strategy">
If your Sanity is dangerously low, swap roles with a teammate. Let someone with high Sanity handle the front desk and CCTV duty while you focus on safer tasks like fetching supplies or cleaning rooms.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Items and Treatment Procedures">

Treating normal patients is how you earn the cash required to buy survival gear. Each room in the hospital requires a specific set of actions and items. Using the wrong item or failing a mini-game can kill the patient, resulting in a loss of rewards and a Sanity penalty.

| Item | Category | Best Use Case | Mistake to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coffee** | Sanity | Restoring mental health | Using when Sanity is >80% |
| **Taser** | Defense | Neutralizing Skinwalkers | Firing at normal patients |
| **Extinguisher** | Emergency | Putting out room fires | Ignoring follow-up treatment |
| **Scalpel** | Surgery | Room 8 surgery tasks | Leaving surgery mid-way |
| **Eye Drops** | Medical | Basic eye treatments | Confusing with Ointment |

Room 8 (Surgery) is the most difficult treatment area. It requires a sequence of timed tool uses. If you start a surgery, you must finish it without leaving the room, or the patient will die instantly. For Room 6 (X-Ray), you must complete a flashing-square pattern on the monitor before applying the final treatment item.

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Item Management">
Inventory space is limited. Always keep one slot open for an emergency item like a Taser or Coffee. Don't fill your pockets entirely with medical supplies if you are the primary anomaly hunter.
</Callout>
