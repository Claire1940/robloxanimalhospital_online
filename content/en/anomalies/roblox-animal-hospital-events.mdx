<Section icon={Target} title="How to Spot and Reject Anomalies">

Anomaly detection is your primary line of defense. When a patient approaches the front desk, you must run through a strict inspection sequence before pressing the accept button. If any red flags appear, use the shutter button immediately to reject them.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="In-Person Visual Check">
    Look directly at the patient at the desk. Scan for physical deformities such as three eyes, hollow sockets, sharp realistic teeth, an impossibly wide grin, or unnatural body twitching.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="CCTV Camera Verification">
    Check the lobby and check-in feed. Some anomalies look normal in person but display black boxes over their eyes, stretched limbs, or completely void-like bodies on camera.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Photo ID Comparison">
    Compare the physical patient with their file photo. Reject them if the photo does not match, appears heavily static-grained, or displays cursed attributes like bloodshot eyes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy the Shutters">
    If any anomaly is confirmed, hit the shutter button. This safely turns the entity away without letting it enter the main hospital corridors.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Inspection Tip">
Establish a set routine with your team: one player dedicated entirely to front desk check-ins and camera feeds, while others handle internal treatment rooms.
</Callout>

</Section>
