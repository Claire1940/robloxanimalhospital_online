<Section icon={Map} title="Roblox Animal Hospital Suitcase: Inventory Mechanics">

Managing your inventory space in the **roblox animal hospital suitcase** system is what separates rookie interns from veteran surgeons. In this survival horror experience, you are tasked with treating infected animal patients while defending yourself against terrifying anomalies. Every item you carry takes up precious space, meaning you must make split-second decisions on what to keep in your hands.

Your default carrying capacity is highly limited, making early shifts exceptionally tense. If you carry too many medical supplies, you won't have room for defensive weapons like the Taser or sanity-restoring consumables like Coffee. Balancing your loadout is essential for keeping both your patients alive and your sanity intact.

| Class Name | Starting Inventory | Max Upgraded Inventory | Primary Role |

| **Intern** | 3 Slots | 3 Slots | Starter / Learning |
| **Nurse** | 4 Slots | 6 Slots | Item Carrier / Support |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Essential Items and Treatment Tools">

To successfully complete a shift, you must understand the exact function of every item that can occupy your inventory slots. Using the wrong tool or giving an incorrect treatment will instantly kill normal patients, lowering your final score and draining your team's collective sanity.

Items in the game are divided into three major categories: Patient Care, Sanity Recovery, and Threat Control. Knowing when to buy these from the shop and how to prioritize them in your suitcase is key to surviving the later, more chaotic shifts.

| Item Name | Category | Primary Use Case | Priority Level | Mistake to Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Coffee** | Sanity | Restores sanity during long shifts | **Critical** | Drinking it too early before sanity drops |
| **X-Taser** | Defense | Stun missed anomalies and Skinwalkers | **High** | Firing blindly and wasting charges |
| **Ointment** | Treatment | Heals burns on patients after fires | **Medium** | Applying before extinguishing the fire |
| **Surgery Tools** | Treatment | Required for Room 8 timed mini-games | **High** | Leaving the room mid-surgery |
| **Herbs** | Treatment | Standard cure for early-stage patients | **Low** | Overstocking suitcase slots with them |

<Callout type="warning" title="Sanity Hazard">
Staring at cursed photos or failing to reject anomalies through the reception shutters will rapidly drain your sanity. Keep a cup of Coffee in your suitcase to recover immediately when these events occur.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Survival Shift Guide">

Surviving a night shift requires a structured routine. Follow this step-by-step workflow to maximize your efficiency, manage your suitcase space, and prevent anomalies from breaching the facility.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Front Desk">
    Begin your shift at the reception area. Your primary goal is to inspect incoming patients. Check their physical features, cross-reference their photos, and monitor the CCTV feed to spot anomalies before letting them in.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Filter Anomalies with Shutters">
    If a patient exhibits strange behavior, realistic teeth, or distorted limbs, immediately press the shutter button to reject them. Admitting an anomaly will spawn a hostile Skinwalker inside the clinic.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Organize Treatment Suitcase">
    Once safe patients are admitted, coordinate with your team. Fill your suitcase with the specific diagnostic tools, herbs, or medical items required for active treatment rooms (Rooms 1-8).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Room Tasks Quickly">
    Complete the interactive mini-games in each room. For Room 6 (X-Ray) and Room 7 (Heart Monitor), follow the screen prompts carefully. Do not click the hazard skulls, as they delay your progress.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Manage Mid-Shift Emergencies">
    If a room catches fire, drop your non-essential treatment items, grab the Fire Extinguisher, clear the flames, and apply Ointment to the injured patient immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Team Coordination">
In multiplayer lobbies, assign one player exclusively to front desk duty with a suitcase focused on defensive items, while other players specialize in treatment and speed-running room tasks.
</Callout>

</Section>
