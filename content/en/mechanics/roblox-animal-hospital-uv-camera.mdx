To survive the late-game shifts, you must follow a strict inspection checklist for every single visitor. Rushing through check-ins is the most common cause of early run failures.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Visual Inspection">
    Look at the patient directly through the reception glass. Check for obvious signs like twitching, wide grins, or extra limbs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Perform the UV Camera Scan">
    Equip your UV camera and scan the patient's face and chest. Look for glowing green symbols, hidden third eyes, or realistic teeth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Cross-Reference the Photo ID">
    Compare the live patient with their official photo. Check if the ears, nose, or mouth shapes match perfectly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Check the CCTV Feed">
    Switch to the lobby camera. Look for black boxes over the eyes, completely black bodies, or limbs that appear stretched out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Make Your Final Decision">
    If any check fails, press the shutter button to reject the anomaly. If all checks pass, admit them to a treatment room.
  </Step>
</Steps>
